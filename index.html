AMRO EL-TETAWY MUSEUM

The Amro El-Tetawy Museum is dedicated to the work of the Austrian-Egyptian filmmaker Amro El-Tetawy.

The Museum

Founded in 2026, the Amro El-Tetawy Museum serves both as an exhibition space and as a site of artistic production across film, visual art, music and performance. The museum was founded by cultural scholar Philipp Gönitzer, who also acts as its director and curator. The building was designed and constructed by the lawyer and art patron Siegfried Rack. Several of El-Tetawy’s early films were produced there, including “1. Aufgabe”, “2 Aufgabe”, “Unicorn House”, and “Die Passion der Erdbeere”. Therefore the site carries a direct connection to the artist’s work and functions simultaneously as exhibition space and historical context.

Collection and Program

The museum’s collection includes films, photographs, and sculptures by Amro El-Tetawy as well as works by artists whose practices resonate with his artistic approach, including Günter Brus, Otto Mühl, Gustav Januš, Elena Fekonja, Philipp fun Gonicer, and Richard Kaplenig. The program includes solo and group exhibitions as well as film screenings, live music and performance. Through this interdisciplinary approach, the museum creates a platform for dialogue between different artistic forms.

Biography

Amro El-Tetawy was born on May 24, 2000, in Ain-Shams, Egypt. In the same year, his family emigrated to Austria, where he later lived primarily in Carinthia and Vienna. He attended Federal Secondary School Klagenfurt with a focus on music before enrolling at the Vienna Film School. During this period he produced several early short films including “1. Aufgabe”, “2 Aufgabe”, and “Unicorn House”. After refusing to submit “Unicorn House” as his diploma film—arguing that the faculty was not qualified to judge the work—he left the institution without graduating. His application to the Vienna Film Academy with these films resulted in a legal dispute after director Michael Haneke filed a complaint concerning the use of a swastika in two of the works. Following an arrest and investigation of El-Tetawy, the case was dismissed. In the following years El-Tetawy largely withdrew from the film industry. In 2025 he founded the production company Tetawy Films, through which he now produces his own movies.

Curatorial Statement

The artist is the outlet through which God expresses himself, and God is the foundation of all principles of reality. In this sense, Amro El-Tetawy is perhaps the most important artist of his generation and perhaps the greatest African filmmaker of all time. The Amro El-Tetawy Museum was therefore long overdue, and in the name of its namesake, I, as curator, will strive to show nothing but art here.

— Philipp Gönitzer

Visit

The Amro El-Tetawy Museum does not maintain regular public opening hours and its address is not publicly listed. Visitors are generally admitted during public events such as exhibitions, screenings, concerts, or performances. Outside of these occasions, visits are possible only by appointment. Once a visit has been confirmed, the address of the museum will be provided. Guided tours or film screenings from the collection may be arranged upon request. The museum reserves the right to decline visit requests.
